# Logo brief: Lighthouse AAC app icon + wordmark

Hand this to the designer. Attach the KHF brand files alongside it
(see "Attachments" at the bottom).

**Display name is "Lighthouse AAC"** (not bare "Lighthouse"). The App
Store has many apps named "Lighthouse," so the wordmark must be built
around "Lighthouse AAC." Bundle ID `org.kinderhorizon.lighthouse` is
unrelated and fixed.

## What it is

A tablet app that gives non-speaking children a grid of picture-symbols
they tap to speak aloud. Free, open-source, privacy-first (no ads, no
tracking, all data stays on the device). Built by the Kinder Horizon
Foundation, a children's nonprofit. Used by families and by speech-
language clinicians. It is an educational / communication tool, not a
toy and not a medical device.

## Core idea

A lighthouse as a beacon of communication: a warm guiding light that
helps a child be heard and find their way. The light is the hero.
"Kinder Horizon" evokes a hopeful horizon, so a lighthouse over a calm
horizon line also ties the app to its parent foundation.

## Mood

Warm, hopeful, calm, trustworthy, gentle. Friendly enough for a
3-year-old, dignified enough to sit on a clinician's iPad. Not
cartoonish or babyish, not cold or clinical, not corporate-techy.

## Color (use the actual KHF palette)

- Amber (signature, the "light"): `#E8873C`. Also the color of the
  in-app glow that guides the child to the next word.
- Amber deep (accent/shadow): `#C8631E`.
- Horizon teal (the calm harbor / ground / sea-and-sky): `#6FA9B8`,
  deeper `#4F8A99`. This is the natural partner to the amber light.
- Sage (optional): `#8BA888`.
- Cream / paper (backgrounds): `#F6EFE4`, `#FBF7EE`.
- Ink (darkest, outlines/text): `#1F3A44`.

Keep the mark to 2-3 colors. Amber light against horizon teal is the
intended hero contrast.

## Concept directions (pick or blend)

1. A simple lighthouse with a warm gold beam; the beam stylized as a
   soft glow or gentle rays (a nod to the app's "glow" guidance).
2. The lighthouse light itself as the focal mark: a warm gold beacon
   radiating outward.
3. A lighthouse over a single horizon line (visual tie to "Kinder
   Horizon").

## Must-haves

- Works as an app icon from 1024px down to 29px: bold, simple
  silhouette, no fine detail, no text inside the mark.
- **The lighthouse must be unmistakably a lighthouse at 29px.** A beam
  stylized as "rays" is the single most common way lighthouse logos
  fail: at small sizes they read as a radio tower, wifi symbol, or
  podcast icon. The tower structure must carry the recognition, with
  the light as support, not the reverse.
- **Survives Apple's iOS 18 tinted / dark monochrome icon treatment.**
  The mark must still read when flattened to a single tint. (We need a
  clean 1-color version anyway, so design for it from the start.)
- Legible in pure 1-color and on both light and dark backgrounds.
- High contrast, clean rounded shapes. The audience includes children
  with visual-processing and motor differences, so clarity beats
  decoration.

## Avoid

Text or letters inside the icon; speech-bubble cliches (unless very
subtle); medical crosses or clinical symbols (this is not a medical
device); national flags or culture-specific markers; gradients that
vanish at small sizes; busy scenes.

## Deliverables

- Vector source (SVG / AI).
- Full-bleed 1024x1024 icon (no transparency, no pre-baked rounded
  corners; the stores add those).
- A horizontal lockup: mark + "Lighthouse AAC" wordmark, for the website
  and the in-app About screen.
- 1-color and reversed-white versions.
- A version that survives the iOS 18 tinted-icon mode.

Should sit comfortably next to the Kinder Horizon Foundation brand.
